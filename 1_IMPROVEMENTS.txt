I started with ChromaDB because it was easy to prototype with. As the project evolved, I migrated to Redis Stack.
 My assistant retrieves SEC filings based on metadata like company, filing type, and year before performing vector search, 
 and Redis supports efficient metadata filtering together with HNSW vector indexing. It also gives me a production-ready 
 datastore that I can later use for retrieval caching and conversational state, so I don't need separate systems for 
 vectors and caching. The retrieval quality still 
comes from my embeddings and cross-encoder reranker, while Redis improves the serving infrastructure and scalability.
